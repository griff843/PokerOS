## hu_01
### SRP Bluffcatch River
- Check board texture: paired, monotone, four-liner?
- Count combos of value hands villain can have
- Count combos of missed draws / bluffs villain can have
- Compare pot odds to required bluffcatch frequency
- Consider population tendency (Pool B: underbluffs big rivers)
- Adjust for paired top card reducing villain value combos
- Look for scare cards (ace on river) that shift ranges

## hu_02
### Facing Turn Overbet
- Identify overbet sizing (>75% pot)
- Assess range polarization: villain is repping nuts or air
- Evaluate your hand's relative strength vs polarized range
- Consider board texture changes from flop to turn
- Pool B tends to under-bluff overbets — lean toward folding marginal hands
- Check if turn card completes obvious draws
- Identify if you block villain's value hands

## hu_03
### River Raise as Bluff
- Identify boards where you can credibly rep the nuts
- Check for missed draws in your range that have zero showdown value
- Assess if villain's bet sizing suggests a capped range
- Consider four-liner and flush-completing rivers as bluff opportunities
- Pool B under-calls river raises — exploit with thinner bluffs
- Size raise to maximize fold equity while risking minimum
- Avoid bluff-raising when villain is uncapped

## hu_04
### Turn Probe Bet
- Missed c-bet flop IP — OOP can probe turn
- Look for turn cards that favor OOP range (e.g., flush completes)
- Size probe 50-66% pot on polar turns
- Target hands that checked back flop with marginal equity
- Consider whether villain would have c-bet strong hands on flop
- Pool B under-defends vs probes — exploit with wider betting range
- Be cautious probing into dry board textures

## hu_05
### Check-Raise Flop SRP
- Identify wet/connected flop textures that favor check-raising
- Build check-raise range with draws + sets + two-pair
- Include enough bluffs (gutshots, backdoors) for balance
- Size check-raise to 3x the flop bet for value
- Consider that Pool B under-defends vs flop check-raises
- Avoid check-raising dry boards where c-bettor has range advantage
- Plan for turn and river lines after check-raise gets called

## hu_06
### Triple Barrel Bluff
- Requires credible line: preflop aggressor who c-bet flop and turn
- Choose bluffs that block villain's continuing range
- Fire third barrel on scare cards (ace, paired board, four-liner)
- Size river bet large (75-100% pot) for maximum pressure
- Pool B over-folds to triple barrels — high-frequency exploit
- Avoid triple barreling when board is static and villain is uncapped
- Assess if turn bet got called by draws that now missed

## hu_07
### Facing River Donk Bet
- Donk bet = villain bets into preflop/previous street aggressor
- Usually polarized: strong hand or pure bluff
- Assess sizing: small donk is often blocking bet, large is polarized
- Consider what changed on the river to motivate the donk
- Pool B uses donk bets infrequently — weight toward value when seen
- Raising a donk bet is high EV when you have the nuts
- With bluffcatchers, call small donks and fold to large ones

## hu_08
### Turn Delayed C-Bet
- Checked back flop in position, now betting turn
- Turn card should favor your range or change equity distribution
- Flush-completing turns and overcards are good delayed c-bet cards
- Size 50-66% pot as delayed c-bet
- Pool B under-defends vs delayed c-bets — exploit wide
- Strong for hands that wanted to pot control flop but improved on turn
- Avoid delayed c-bet on blank turn cards that don't change anything

## hu_09
### River Value Bet Sizing
- Assess villain's calling range and its elasticity
- Overbet (>pot) when you have the nuts and villain has clear second-best hands
- Bet 50-66% pot with medium-strength value that wants calls from worse
- Paired top card on river narrows villain's calling range
- Pool B calls too wide vs small river bets — thin value bet small
- Consider if overbet opportunity exists when you have nut advantage
- Avoid value betting when villain's range is mostly better or folding

## hu_10
### Facing Flop Check-Raise
- Assess flop texture: wet boards have more legitimate check-raises
- With strong hands (overpair+), usually call and evaluate turn
- Folding marginal hands is fine vs Pool B check-raises (less bluffs)
- Consider 3-betting the check-raise with very strong hands and bluffs
- Look for turn cards that would give you overbet opportunities
- Pool B check-raises are heavily weighted toward value
- With drawing hands, call if odds are correct and fold if not
